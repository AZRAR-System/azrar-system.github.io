How to Calculate Late Rent Fees?

Three fair ways to calculate the fee, with practical examples and legal tips.

June 8, 2026 · 4 min read

How to Calculate Late Rent Fees Fairly

A late fee protects your rights as an owner or agency, but it must be fair and clear so it doesn't become a source of dispute. Here are three common ways to calculate it with examples.

1. Fixed fee

A fixed amount added when an installment is late, regardless of how long. Example: 20 JOD per late installment. Simple, but can be harsh for short delays.

2. Daily fee

An amount or rate per day of delay. Example: 1 JOD/day. The fairest as it matches the actual delay — which is why accurate actual-day calculation (28/29/30/31) matters.

3. Percentage fee

A percentage of the overdue installment (e.g. 2%). Scales with the amount and suits larger contracts.

Legal tip: State the late-fee method clearly in the contract and keep a reasonable cap — excessive fees may be struck down in court.
